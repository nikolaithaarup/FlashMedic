# FlashMedic content standard

This standard applies to editorial review of FlashMedic flashcards. It defines a writing and safety baseline; it does not replace clinical guidelines, regional instructions or subject-matter review.

## Language and format

- Write idiomatic, professional Danish for ambulance students and prehospital clinicians.
- Prefer a question of roughly 5-20 words and a concise answer that can usually be read in one breath. Use `explanation` or `rationale` for nuance rather than making `answer` a textbook paragraph.
- Avoid literal English syntax, unnecessary nominalisations and repeated openings. Vary direct recall, comparison, mechanism, cause-and-effect, interpretation and short scenarios.
- Use accepted abbreviations such as IV, IM and PO only when they are common in Danish ambulance education and unambiguous in context. Define less familiar abbreviations on first use.
- A short scenario should contain only details needed for the learning objective. Ask for interpretation or the underlying principle, not a definitive diagnosis from insufficient information.

## Educational design

- Test one main learning objective per card. Split a card if its parts could reasonably be learned or answered independently.
- Merge or remove cards that test the same objective without adding a useful new context. Cross-topic repetition is acceptable only when the context changes what the learner must reason about.
- Use factual recall for terminology, core relationships and safety-critical distinctions. Prefer mechanism or clinical reasoning when understanding improves transfer to patient assessment.
- Do not reveal the answer through wording, unequal alternatives or repeated key phrases.
- Preserve an existing ID when language or accuracy is improved without changing the learning objective. Never reuse an existing ID for a different objective.

## Clinical-safety language

- Treat `altid`, `aldrig`, `skal`, `må ikke`, `kontraindiceret`, `diagnostisk`, `førstevalg`, `hyppigste` and `mest almindelige` as review signals. Keep them only when the scope and source justify the claim.
- Prefer calibrated wording such as `typisk`, `kan`, `taler for`, `kræver vurdering` or `afhænger af` when findings or effects are variable.
- Exact doses, concentrations, treatment thresholds and time intervals require an identifiable, current source and manual review for the intended population and setting.
- Route-specific administration, contraindications, paediatric dosing, resuscitation treatment and recommendations tied to a guideline or regional protocol require manual review even when the wording is retained.
- If a claim cannot be supported confidently, rewrite it as a stable general principle or flag it. Do not invent a citation or imply local authority.

## Pharmacology terminology

| Term | Preferred use |
|---|---|
| Lægemiddel | The finished medicinal product or a substance used to prevent, diagnose or treat disease. Prefer over the broad colloquial `medicin` in technical explanations. |
| Aktivt stof | The component responsible for the pharmacological effect. |
| Farmakokinetik | What the body does to the active substance: absorption, distribution, metabolism and elimination. |
| Farmakodynamik | What the active substance does to the body, including target interaction, effect and dose-response relationships. |
| Agonist | A ligand that binds to and activates a receptor. Qualify partial agonists when relevant. |
| Antagonist | A ligand that reduces or blocks receptor activation without itself producing the same receptor response. |
| Affinitet | Tendency or strength of binding between a ligand and its target; do not equate it with clinical effect. |
| Effekt | The response produced; use `maksimal effekt` when discussing efficacy. |
| Potens | The amount or concentration needed to produce a specified effect; do not use as a synonym for maximal effect. |
| Biotilgængelighed | The fraction of an administered dose that reaches the systemic circulation unchanged. |
| Førstepassagemetabolisme | Presystemic metabolism, especially in gut wall and liver, before an orally administered substance reaches systemic circulation. |
| Halveringstid | The time required for plasma concentration or amount in the body to fall by half under the stated kinetic assumptions. |
| Distribution | Reversible transfer of a substance between blood and tissues. |
| Proteinbinding | Binding, particularly to plasma proteins; the unbound fraction is generally available for distribution, metabolism and target interaction. |
| Metabolisme | Enzymatic conversion to metabolites, often but not exclusively in the liver. |
| Elimination | Removal from the body through metabolism and excretion. |
| Bivirkning | An unintended harmful response at normally used doses; distinguish it from expected pharmacological effects when context requires. |
| Interaktion | A change in effect or exposure caused by another substance, food, disease state or relevant factor. |
| Indikation | The clinical reason or condition for which use is considered appropriate. |
| Kontraindikation | A circumstance in which a treatment should not be used; distinguish absolute from relative and follow current product information or instruction. |
| Administrationsvej | The route by which a medicine is given, for example PO, IV, IM, SC, inhaled or intranasal. |

## Metadata and review status

- `draft`: content is usable for development but has not completed clinical and source review. Language editing alone does not justify `reviewed`.
- `reviewed`: the claim, explanation and reference have been checked; required V2 metadata is complete and the review date identifies when that check occurred.
- `retired`: retained only when historical traceability is needed and the card should not be used for new learning.
- Add optional V2 fields only when they improve learning or safety. Keep `answer` concise; place mechanism and nuance in `explanation` or `rationale`.
- `commonMistakes`, `examTip` and `redFlag` should state a concrete misconception or safety issue, not repeat the answer.
- References must be real, identifiable sources. Local instructions take precedence for operational treatment decisions.

## Manual-review triggers

Record the card ID, original wording, risk, action and residual uncertainty when a card contains an exact dose or threshold, route-specific instruction, contraindication, protocol-dependent treatment, paediatric or pregnancy detail, resuscitation recommendation, unsupported frequency claim, possible factual error or unresolved duplicate objective.
